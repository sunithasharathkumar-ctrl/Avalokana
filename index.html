The Journey

"What you seek is also seeking you." — Rumi

This cultural philosophy narrates the journey of a hero named Madana (meaning "king of lust"), chosen by a monk (Swamiji) to succeed him as the next head of a monastery. Swamiji gave him a task to prove his worthiness: he must undertake a barefoot pilgrimage to a distant temple to seek divine permission for his next big initiation.

The Swamiji imposes the significant responsibility on him to maintain purity and avoid moral pitfalls (Arishadvargas = Lust, Anger, Greed, Infatuation, Self-Pride, Jealousy) during his journey. As a man who has sheltered like a frog in a well for so many years, the unfamiliar characters and experiences of the outside world begin to open up.

This curious spiritual journey explores Madana's internal struggle with purity, responsibility, cognitive transformation, and self-discovery.

Facing the challenge of maintaining his integrity, the narrative questions whether he will retain control or succumb to temptations, ultimately reflecting on the lessons learned. That is Avalokana, meaning "Self Realization".

The Creators

The minds behind the meditative vision

Vishal Kampli

Madana (The Seeker)

In a highly demanding spiritual role, Vishal Kampli portrays Madana (meaning "king of lust"), a sheltered young man chosen by his Swamiji to succeed him. Vishal delivers a powerful performance detailing Madana's internal cognitive struggle against the Arishadvargas (the six moral pitfalls) in the outside world, making his path of self-discovery feel deeply relatable.

"Embodying Madana's transition from a naive monk-in-training to a man facing the temptations of the real world was a profound spiritual journey for me."

Rakshith Shivakumar

Writer & Director

"Avalokana is my soul project. Obviously it is my first, but I will definitely bring a creative transformational wave in my future movies." Rakshith Shivakumar infuses his debut feature with intense philosophical depth, exploring cognitive transformation and self-realization with a fresh, visionary approach to Indian art cinema.

"Avalokana is a mirror of self-realization. The journey is not outward, but inward."
